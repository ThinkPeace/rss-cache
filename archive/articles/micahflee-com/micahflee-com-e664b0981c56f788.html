As part of this release, we also published a searchable version of the dataset using Google Pinpoint, a Google product for journalists. Pinpoint is nice and easy to use. It lets you index large folders of documents that are in Google Drive – this Epstein dataset is about 40GB – and it makes them searchable. It also does entity extraction, allowing you to easily filter by names of people and organization, and by locations and timestamps.

💡
Google Pinpoint is Google product. Only use Pinpoint for datasets when you don't mind sharing a copy of your data with Google. It's great for completely public documents like these, but I recommend against using it for private data. Instead, use a tool like Aleph for that.

If you're interested in searching the latest Epstein files yourself, give it a shot.
