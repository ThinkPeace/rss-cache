Chess Peace — a new iOS game by Sam Shepherd — is my kind of logic puzzle. Each puzzle is a board with a few unplaced chess pieces. To solve you need to place all the pieces so that none of them attack each other. There’s a timer if you care, but I don’t. (And you can hide the clock.) Clever name too: the pieces need to be ... at peace with each other. You…

Place every piece so none can capture another.

26,500+ unique puzzles across 5 distinct modes.
Available now for free on the App Store.

Simple rules, thousands of puzzles

Each piece attacks just like in real chess. A built-in reference shows you every move pattern with one tap.

Tap or drag pieces from the tray onto the board. Place every piece so none can capture another. Undo, reset, or use one of three hints anytime — no penalty for taking your time.

Every solve records your time and move count. Work through over 25,000 puzzles across a range of difficulties and modes.

5 distinct challenges

The original challenge. Place one of each piece so none attack each other. Pure spatial reasoning on boards from 4×4 to 6×6.

Multiple copies of the same piece must coexist alongside the others. Poker's not the only game with a pair of queens!

White and black pieces together in harmony. Same colour pieces are allies; opposite are enemies.

The board is split into disconnected regions. Pieces only threaten others on their island.

Some positions are revealed. Figure out which pieces belong where.
